## Major Issues (Must Fix)

### 1. English Grammar Error + Chinese Error (Seed S0101, LEGO L03)
**Phrase:** "I'd like this language" → "我想这个语言"
**Complexity:** 4 LEGOs
**Problems:**
- **English:** Ungrammatical - "I'd like this language" is incomplete. Needs a verb: "I'd like to learn/speak/study this language"
- **Chinese:** Wrong structure - "我想这个语言" is incorrect. 想 (xiǎng) requires a verb phrase or clause after it, not just a noun. Should be "我想学这个语言" (I want to learn this language) or "我喜欢这个语言" (I like this language)

**Impact:** High - This teaches incorrect grammar in both languages

---

### 2. Chinese Verb Error (Seed S0102, LEGO L03)
**Phrase:** "If you think it's not like that" → "如果你想它不是那样的"
**Complexity:** 6 LEGOs
**Problem:**
- **Chinese:** Wrong verb - "想" (xiǎng) means "want/would like," not "think." Should use "认为" (rènwéi) or "觉得" (juéde) for "think"
- Correct: "如果你认为它不是那样的" or "如果你觉得它不是那样的"

**Impact:** High - Fundamental verb meaning error

---

### 3. English Grammar Error (Seed S0103, LEGO L04) - Multiple instances
**Phrase 1:** "We're trying to say many more about this" → "我们正在试图说更多的关于这"
**Phrase 2:** "I'm enjoying finding out many more about what we're doing" → "我很享受了解更多的关于我们正在做的"
**Complexity:** 5-6 LEGOs
**Problem:**
- **English:** "Many more" without a noun is incomplete/awkward. Should be "many more things" or just "more"
- The Chinese translations add 的 which implies there should be a noun, making it clearer that something is missing

**Impact:** High - Ungrammatical English in longer phrases

## Moderate Issues (Should Fix)

### 4. Awkward English Construction (Seed S0102, LEGO L04)
**Phrase:** "You shouldn't worry if it's not like that" → "你不应该担心如果它不是那样的"
**Complexity:** 5 LEGOs
**Problem:**
- **English:** "Worry if" is awkward. Better: "worry about whether it's like that" or "worry that it's not like that"
- **Chinese:** Direct translation creates similar awkwardness

**Impact:** Moderate - Comprehensible but unnatural

---

### 5. Awkward Chinese Verb Choice (Seed S0103, LEGO L02) - 2 instances
**Phrase 1:** "I'm trying to hear you say this" → "我正在试图听你说这"
**Phrase 2:** "We're not trying to hear you say that" → "我们不是在试图听你说那样的"
**Complexity:** 4-5 LEGOs
**Problem:**
- **Chinese:** "试图听" (shìtú tīng = trying to hear) + person + verb is awkward/unnatural
- Better alternatives: "想听" (xiǎng tīng = want to hear), "试着听" (shìzhe tīng = try to hear), or "努力听" (nǔlì tīng = try hard to hear)
- "试图" is too formal/literary for this context

**Impact:** Moderate - Native speakers would understand but would choose different words

---

### 6. Incomplete Chinese Phrase (Seed S0103, LEGO L04)
**Phrase:** "I need a little more time for many more" → "我需要多一点时间为了更多的"
**Complexity:** 5 LEGOs
**Problem:**
- **Chinese:** "为了更多的" ends with the particle 的, which requires a noun after it. The phrase is incomplete.
- Should be "为了更多的东西/事情" (for more things) or restructured

**Impact:** Moderate - Grammatically incomplete

### 8. Incomplete Conditional (Seed S0101, LEGO L03)
**Phrase:** "If you can speak this language slowly" → "如果你能说这个语言慢"
**Complexity:** 6 LEGOs
**Problem:**
- **Both languages:** This is a conditional clause without a consequence. It's a fragment.
- **Chinese:** Also has word order issue - "慢" (slowly) should come before the verb: "如果你能慢慢说这个语言" or after as "慢慢地"

**Impact:** Minor - Functions as a teaching fragment but technically incomplete

---

### 9. Word Order Issues (Seed S0120)
**Phrase:** "You like when we talk to each other" → "你喜欢我们互相交谈时"
**Complexity:** 4 LEGOs
**Problem:**

**Impact:** Minor - Comprehensible but could be clearer
